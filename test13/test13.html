WAEC Chemistry Practical - Test 13

Corrected market-test paper: ethanoic acid against impure KOH, and C as lead(II) carbonate plus starch.

Specimens

A

\(0.100\,mol\,dm^{-3}\) \(CH_3COOH\).

B

\(6.0\,g\,dm^{-3}\) impure \(KOH\).

C

A mixture of lead(II) carbonate, \(PbCO_3\), and starch.

C is not a copper salt in this paper. Treat it as lead(II) carbonate plus starch throughout.

Question 1 - Quantitative Analysis

A is \(0.100\,mol\,dm^{-3}\) \(CH_3COOH\). B is \(6.0\,g\,dm^{-3}\) impure \(KOH\).

Put A in the burette and titrate it against \(25.0\,cm^3\) of B using methyl orange as indicator. Repeat the titration to obtain concordant titre values. Tabulate your results and calculate the average volume of A used.

\[CH_3COOH(aq)+KOH(aq)\rightarrow CH_3COOK(aq)+H_2O(l)\]

[H = 1.0, C = 12.0, K = 39.0, O = 16.0, \(N_A=6.02\times10^{23}\)]

From your results and the information provided, calculate:

  1. the concentration of KOH in B in \(mol\,dm^{-3}\);
  2. the concentration of KOH in B in \(g\,dm^{-3}\);
  3. the percentage impurity in B;
  4. the amount in moles of ethanoic acid in the average volume of A used;
  5. the number of water molecules formed in the reaction.

Question 2 - Qualitative Analysis

C is a mixture of an inorganic salt and an organic compound. Perform the following tests on C. Record observations and identify any gas evolved. State conclusions in a table.

  1. Put all of C in a beaker and add about \(5\,cm^3\) of dilute \(HNO_3\). Stir and filter. Keep the residue and divide the filtrate into two portions.
  2. To the first portion of the filtrate, add \(NaOH(aq)\) in drops and then in excess.
  3. To the second portion, add \(NH_3(aq)\) in drops and then in excess.
  4. To the residue, add about \(2\,cm^3\) of distilled water. Boil the mixture and add a few drops of iodine solution.

Question 3 - Practical Knowledge

  1. Complete: apparatus for measuring a specific volume of liquid; use of clay triangle; apparatus for melting and heating to very high temperature; use of bomb calorimeter.
  2. Arrange \(PbCl_2\), \(PbSO_4\) and \(PbNO_3\) in increasing order of solubility.
  3. State the effect on litmus of \(KNO_3(aq)\), \(K_2CO_3(aq)\) and \(Al_2(SO_4)_3(aq)\).

Question 1 Solution

Final reading / \(cm^3\)25.2024.8524.8024.85
Initial reading / \(cm^3\)0.000.000.000.00
Titre / \(cm^3\)25.2024.8524.8024.85
\[Average=\frac{24.85+24.80+24.85}{3}=24.83\,cm^3\]
\[n(CH_3COOH)=\frac{0.100\times24.83}{1000}=2.483\times10^{-3}\,mol\]

The mole ratio is \(CH_3COOH:KOH=1:1\), so moles of \(KOH\) in \(25.0\,cm^3\) of B \(=2.483\times10^{-3}\,mol\).

\[C_B=\frac{2.483\times10^{-3}\times1000}{25.0}=0.0993\,mol\,dm^{-3}\]
\[M_r(KOH)=39+16+1=56\]
\[Mass\ concentration=0.0993\times56=5.56\,g\,dm^{-3}\]
\[%\ impurity=\frac{6.00-5.56}{6.00}\times100=7.33\%\]
\[Number\ of\ H_2O\ molecules=2.483\times10^{-3}\times6.02\times10^{23}=1.49\times10^{21}\]
Teacher point: because the reaction is 1:1, the moles of acid used equal the moles of KOH neutralized and also equal the moles of water formed.
