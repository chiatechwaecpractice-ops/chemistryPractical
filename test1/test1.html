WAEC Chemistry Practical - Test 1

Three-question practice: acid-carbonate titration, ammonium salt analysis and practical apparatus.

Specimens

A is \(0.100\,mol\,dm^{-3}\) \(HCl\). B is hydrated sodium trioxocarbonate(IV), \(Na_2CO_3\cdot xH_2O\), containing \(14.30\,g\,dm^{-3}\) of solution. C is a mixture of \(NH_4Cl\) and \(CaCO_3\).

Question 1 - Quantitative Analysis

Put A in the burette. Pipette \(25.0\,cm^3\) of B into a conical flask. Add 2 to 3 drops of methyl orange and titrate to a permanent orange endpoint. Repeat to obtain concordant titres.

\[Na_2CO_3(aq)+2HCl(aq)\rightarrow2NaCl(aq)+CO_2(g)+H_2O(l)\]
  1. Tabulate your readings.
  2. Calculate average titre.
  3. Calculate concentration of \(Na_2CO_3\) in B.
  4. Calculate molar mass of the hydrated salt.
  5. Calculate \(x\) in \(Na_2CO_3\cdot xH_2O\).

Question 2 - Qualitative Analysis

Perform tests on C. Record observations and inferences.

  1. Add distilled water to C, shake and filter.
  2. Warm a portion of the filtrate with \(NaOH(aq)\).
  3. Test the gas with moist red litmus paper.
  4. Add dilute \(HCl\) to the residue.
  5. Pass any gas into lime water.

Question 3 - Practical Knowledge

  1. State two precautions in titration.
  2. Why is methyl orange suitable for acid-carbonate titration?
  3. Draw a labelled filtration setup.
  4. State one use each of burette, pipette and conical flask.

\[Average=24.78\,cm^3\]
\[n(HCl)=\frac{0.100\times24.78}{1000}=2.478\times10^{-3}\,mol\]
\[n(Na_2CO_3)=\frac{1}{2}\times2.478\times10^{-3}=1.239\times10^{-3}\,mol\]
\[C_B=\frac{1.239\times10^{-3}\times1000}{25.0}=0.0496\,mol\,dm^{-3}\]
\[M_r=\frac{14.30}{0.0496}=288.3\]
\[106+18x=288.3\]
\[x=\frac{288.3-106}{18}=10.1\approx10\]

Therefore, B is approximately \(Na_2CO_3\cdot10H_2O\).
