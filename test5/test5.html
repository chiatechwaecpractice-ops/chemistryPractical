WAEC Chemistry Practical - Test 5

Three-question practice: ethanoic acid titration, aluminium salt analysis and exam theory.

Specimens

A is \(0.100\,mol\,dm^{-3}\) \(CH_3COOH\). B is \(NaOH\). C is \(Al_2(SO_4)_3\) mixed with starch.

Question 1 - Quantitative Analysis

Put A in the burette. Pipette \(25.0\,cm^3\) of B into the flask. Add phenolphthalein and titrate until the pink colour just disappears.

\[CH_3COOH(aq)+NaOH(aq)\rightarrow CH_3COONa(aq)+H_2O(l)\]
  1. Tabulate results.
  2. Calculate average titre.
  3. Calculate concentration of B.
  4. Calculate mass concentration of NaOH in B.
  5. Calculate number of moles of water formed.

Question 2 - Qualitative Analysis

  1. Add water to C, shake and filter if necessary.
  2. To filtrate add \(NaOH\) in drops and excess.
  3. To another portion add \(NH_3\) in drops and excess.
  4. To another portion add \(BaCl_2\), then dilute \(HCl\).
  5. To residue add water, boil and add iodine.

Question 3 - Practical Knowledge

  1. Why is phenolphthalein suitable for weak acid-strong base titration?
  2. State the confirmatory test for starch.
  3. Give two uses of a wash bottle.
  4. State the effect of \(Al_2(SO_4)_3(aq)\) on litmus.
