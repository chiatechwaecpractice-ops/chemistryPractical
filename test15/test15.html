WAEC Chemistry Practical - Test 15

Set 2 strengthening practice for A1 preparation using the D, E and F reagent set.

Focus of This Practice

This test trains candidates to handle the same reagents with a different question emphasis: titre accuracy, mole ratio, sulphate confirmation, displacement by zinc and clean observation language.

Question 1 - Redox Titration

E is \(KMnO_4\). D is ethanedioic acid. Put E in the burette and titrate it against \(20.0\,cm^3\) or \(25.0\,cm^3\) of D as directed by your centre, after acidifying with dilute \(H_2SO_4\) and warming.

  1. Prepare a complete titration table.
  2. Calculate the average titre if the accurate titres are \(20.35\,cm^3\), \(20.40\,cm^3\), and \(20.35\,cm^3\).
  3. If \(20.0\,cm^3\) of D was used and \(C_D=0.0500\,mol\,dm^{-3}\), calculate \(C_E\).
  4. Calculate the mass concentration of E in \(g\,dm^{-3}\).
  5. Explain why \(KMnO_4\) is a self-indicator.

Question 2 - Qualitative Analysis

F contains a copper(II) salt and starch. Carry out confirmatory tests for the cation, anion and organic component.

  1. Add water to F and filter.
  2. Test the residue with iodine.
  3. Test portions of the filtrate with NaOH, NH3, BaCl2 plus HCl, Pb(NO3)2 and zinc dust.

Question 3 - Practical Theory

  1. State two precautions in permanganate titration.
  2. Why should the flask be swirled during titration?
  3. Why is dilute \(HNO_3\) used before some precipitation tests?
  4. State the colour of \(Cu(OH)_2\).
  5. Describe how to identify starch in F.

Best Solution

Question 1

\[Average=\frac{20.35+20.40+20.35}{3}=20.37\,cm^3\]
\[n(C_2O_4^{2-})=\frac{0.0500\times20.0}{1000}=1.00\times10^{-3}\,mol\]
\[n(MnO_4^-)=\frac{2}{5}\times1.00\times10^{-3}=4.00\times10^{-4}\,mol\]
\[C_E=\frac{4.00\times10^{-4}\times1000}{20.37}=1.96\times10^{-2}\,mol\,dm^{-3}\]
\[Mass\ concentration=0.0196\times158=3.10\,g\,dm^{-3}\]

Teacher: The mole ratio is the heart of this calculation. From the equation, 2 moles of \(MnO_4^-\) react with 5 moles of ethanedioate.
