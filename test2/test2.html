WAEC Chemistry Practical - Test 2

Three-question practice: sulphuric acid and sodium hydroxide, zinc ion analysis and endpoint skill.

Specimens

A is dilute \(H_2SO_4\). B is \(0.100\,mol\,dm^{-3}\) \(NaOH\). C is \(ZnSO_4\) mixed with sand.

Question 1 - Quantitative Analysis

Put A in the burette. Pipette \(25.0\,cm^3\) of B into a conical flask, add phenolphthalein and titrate until the pink colour just disappears.

\[H_2SO_4(aq)+2NaOH(aq)\rightarrow Na_2SO_4(aq)+2H_2O(l)\]
  1. Record titre values.
  2. Find average titre.
  3. Calculate concentration of A in \(mol\,dm^{-3}\).
  4. Calculate concentration of A in \(g\,dm^{-3}\).
  5. Calculate mass of \(H_2SO_4\) in \(250\,cm^3\) of A.

Question 2 - Qualitative Analysis

  1. Add water to C, shake and filter.
  2. To filtrate add \(NaOH\) in drops and then excess.
  3. To another portion add \(NH_3\) in drops and then excess.
  4. To another portion add \(BaCl_2\), then dilute \(HCl\).

Question 3 - Practical Knowledge

  1. Why is phenolphthalein used here?
  2. Why should the pipette not be blown out?
  3. State the colour change at endpoint.
  4. Draw the correct titration setup.

Teacher Solution

\[Average=12.48\,cm^3\]
\[\frac{C_A V_A}{C_B V_B}=\frac{1}{2}\]
\[C_A=\frac{0.100\times25.0}{2\times12.48}=0.100\,mol\,dm^{-3}\]
\[Mass\ concentration=0.100\times98=9.80\,g\,dm^{-3}\]
\[Mass\ in\ 250\,cm^3=9.80\times0.250=2.45\,g\]
