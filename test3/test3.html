WAEC Chemistry Practical - Test 3

Three-question practice: purity of sodium carbonate, iron(II) salt tests and careful inference writing.

Specimens

A is \(0.100\,mol\,dm^{-3}\) \(HCl\). B is \(5.00\,g\,dm^{-3}\) impure \(Na_2CO_3\). C is \(FeSO_4\) mixed with \(NaCl\).

Question 1 - Quantitative Analysis

Titrate A from the burette against \(25.0\,cm^3\) of B using methyl orange.

\[Na_2CO_3+2HCl\rightarrow2NaCl+CO_2+H_2O\]
  1. Tabulate readings.
  2. Calculate average titre.
  3. Calculate concentration of pure \(Na_2CO_3\) in B.
  4. Calculate mass concentration of pure \(Na_2CO_3\).
  5. Calculate percentage purity of B.

Question 2 - Qualitative Analysis

  1. Dissolve C in water and divide into portions.
  2. Add \(NaOH\) in drops and excess.
  3. Add \(NH_3\) in drops and excess.
  4. Add \(AgNO_3\), then dilute \(NH_3\).
  5. Add \(BaCl_2\), then dilute \(HCl\).

Question 3 - Practical Knowledge

  1. Why must the rough titre be excluded?
  2. Why does \(Fe^{2+}\) precipitate turn brown on standing?
  3. State the confirmatory test for chloride.
  4. State two causes of high titre.
